If you run a painting, roofing, HVAC, or remodeling crew, you already know the math: every missed estimate call is a customer who calls the next contractor on the list. Voicemail does not save the job — it loses it.

## The hidden cost of a missed call

Industry data puts the average value of a contractor estimate call between **$300 and $4,000** in pipeline. A typical small shop misses **20–35% of inbound calls** during busy weeks. That is rarely a phone-system problem. It is a coverage problem: nobody picks up because everyone is on a ladder.

## What an AI voice agent actually does

A well-built AI voice agent is not a chatbot pretending to be a person. It is a workflow that:

1. **Answers** the call in your business voice within two rings.
2. **Qualifies** the caller — service type, address, urgency, timeline.
3. **Books** an estimate on your calendar (or routes urgent jobs straight to your cell).
4. **Updates your CRM** so the lead is in your pipeline before you finish the job you are on.

Done right, the caller hangs up thinking they spoke to a helpful dispatcher — because functionally, they did.

## Why communications-first matters

A standalone "AI" bolted onto your phone line usually breaks in week two: it does not know your hours, your service area, your pricing tiers, or your CRM. DSX Edge starts at the communications layer (your 3CX phone system) and **layers AI on top of the workflow you already use**. That is the difference between a demo and a deployment.

- **Week 1** — Map your call types and your booking calendar.
- **Week 2** — Build the agent's script, voice, and CRM hooks.
- **Week 3** — Run shadow mode (the agent listens, your team still answers).
- **Week 4** — Cut over for after-hours, then expand to overflow.

By day 30 most contractors recover the cost of the implementation in booked work.
