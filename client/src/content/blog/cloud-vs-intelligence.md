Every business phone vendor now claims to be "cloud-based." That used to mean something — it meant you stopped buying $40,000 boxes for the closet. Today it just means the dial tone lives somewhere else.

## Cloud is infrastructure. Intelligence is the work.

Hosting your PBX in the cloud:

- Removes hardware from your office.
- Gives your team mobile and desktop apps.
- Cuts cost vs. legacy carriers (often 40–65%).

That is real value. But it stops at "the phone rings." The actual business work — qualifying the lead, updating the CRM, scheduling the appointment, summarizing the call, escalating the angry customer — still falls on whoever picks up.

## What "intelligence on top" looks like

The DSX Edge layer adds, **on top of your existing cloud phone system**:

- AI that answers in your brand voice.
- Routing that understands "I need a quote for a kitchen remodel in 90210."
- CRM updates fired from the call itself.
- After-hours coverage that doesn't sound like a robot.
- Call summaries delivered to the right inbox.

None of this requires throwing away your current setup. It sits on top of it.

## The mental model

> Cloud is electricity. Intelligence is the appliance.

Having electricity in the wall is the starting line, not the finish. The same goes for cloud communications: the infrastructure is necessary, but the work happens in the layer above it.

That layer is what DSX Edge builds.
